All Activities

Explore Every Corner

  • Hiking Trails

    Over 20 miles of marked trails wind through cedar canyons, along bluffs, and past ancient rock art.

  • Lake Bailey Swimming

    Cool off in the park's designated swimming area on Lake Bailey, with a sandy beach and easy access.

  • Fishing

    Cast for bass, bream, and catfish from the banks of Lake Bailey or from the park's fishing pier.

  • Camping

    Over 120 campsites from primitive tent spots to full hookup RV pads, plus cozy Mather Lodge cabins.

  • Photography

    Every season brings a different palette — fall color, spring wildflowers, winter ice at Cedar Falls.

  • Picnicking

    Dozens of shaded picnic areas throughout the park — many with stunning canyon views.

  • Nature Center

    Interactive exhibits for all ages covering local wildlife, geology, and the history of the park.

  • Native American Rock Art

    Thousands of petroglyphs left by prehistoric peoples adorn the park's caves.

  • Boating & Kayaking

    Paddleboats and kayaks available for rental on Lake Bailey — no motorized boats permitted.
