Common Questions

FAQ

Is there an entrance fee to visit the park? +
No — day use of Petit Jean State Park is free for all visitors. Fees only apply for overnight stays (camping or lodge/cabin accommodations).
Can I make camping or cabin reservations online? +
Yes. Reservations can be made through the Arkansas State Parks reservation system at arkansasstateparks.com. Summer weekends and fall foliage season book up quickly — reserve at least 2–4 weeks in advance.
Is the Cedar Falls trail open year-round? +
The Cedar Falls trail is generally open year-round, weather permitting. In winter, the trail can become icy and dangerous — the park may temporarily close it after freezing precipitation. Always check trail conditions by calling the visitor center before visiting.
Is there cell service on the mountain? +
Cell service is limited and inconsistent throughout much of the park. We strongly recommend downloading offline maps (AllTrails, Google Maps) before arriving. The lodge and visitor center areas typically have better coverage.
Are campfires allowed? +
Campfires are permitted in designated fire rings at campsites only. During periods of high fire danger, the park may impose temporary fire bans. Firewood is available for purchase at the camp store. Collecting dead wood from the forest is not permitted.
What's the best time of year to see Cedar Falls at peak flow? +
Cedar Falls is most dramatic in late winter and spring (February–April) following seasonal rains. In a dry summer, the flow can slow significantly. The falls also partially freeze in January, creating a stunning ice formation. Fall brings reliable flow plus the bonus of fall foliage color.
Is there a restaurant or food service in the park? +
Mather Lodge offers a full-service restaurant open for breakfast, lunch, and dinner during peak season (spring through fall). Hours are reduced in winter. A camp store near the campground sells basic supplies, firewood, and snacks.
